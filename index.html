The Causes of Climate Change

What is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns. While natural, human activities—primarily the burning of fossil fuels—have been the main driver since the 1800s.

Greenhouse gases act like a blanket wrapped around the Earth, trapping the sun's heat and raising temperatures.

Fossil Fuels & Air Pollution

The primary driver of climate change is the burning of fossil fuels like coal, oil, and gas. This process releases vast amounts of carbon dioxide and nitrous oxide into our atmosphere.

These pollutants act like a thermal blanket, trapping heat and creating the "Greenhouse Effect" that rapidly warms our planet's surface.

Deforestation & Land Use

Forests play a vital role by absorbing CO2 from the air. When trees are cleared for agriculture or industry, that stored carbon is released back into the atmosphere.

Mass deforestation not only reduces the Earth's capacity to "breathe" but also destroys the biodiversity necessary for a resilient global ecosystem.

Industrial Agriculture

Modern farming practices contribute significantly to global warming. Livestock like cows produce methane, a greenhouse gas far more potent than carbon dioxide in the short term.

Additionally, the use of chemical fertilizers releases nitrous oxide, further accelerating the heating of our atmosphere.

Waste & Manufacturing

Our "throwaway culture" fills landfills with waste that decomposes and releases methane gas. The energy-intensive process of manufacturing new products also requires massive amounts of fossil fuels.

By reducing consumption and improving recycling, we can lower the industrial demand that fuels climate change.

About Our Mission

ActNow is a student-led digital initiative at the forefront of climate advocacy. By transforming complex environmental data into accessible visual stories, we aim to bridge the gap between scientific research and community action.


We believe that through a dedicated focus on renewable energy, reforestation, and sustainable lifestyle habits, informed individuals can become the primary drivers of global change. This platform serves as a vital resource hub for those ready to defend our environment and secure a sustainable future for all.
